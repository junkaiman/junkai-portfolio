## Inspiration  
Millions of elderly individuals struggle with identifying their medications, understanding side effects, and navigating complex drug interactions. The inspiration for our project stems from team members witnessing grandparents struggling to understand and use smartphone interfaces, often getting lost in complex navigation and small font sizes. To make things easier for this demographic, our team tried tackling this problem with an intuitive AI-powered app that makes medication management effortless.  

## What It Does  
Users can simply take a photo of their pill, and our app instantly identifies the drug, providing clear and accessible informationâ€”including descriptions, side effects, and potential interactions. We also integrated text-to-speech to read out the medication details, making it easier for users with reading difficulties to access crucial information effortlessly. 

## How We Built It  
We use AI for pill recognition and summarization. The backend crawls medication data from [Drugs.com](https://www.drugs.com), while the front end delivers a seamless user experience. The system is designed for full-stack deployment, ensuring accessibility and efficiency.  

## Challenges We Ran Into  
Understanding the user experience and designing an interface that is intuitive for elderly individuals were key challenges. Ensuring high accuracy in pill recognition and reliable data retrieval also required fine-tuning our AI model and backend system.

## What We Learned  
- The importance of designing for accessibility, especially for elderly users.  
- Optimizing AI for real-world pill recognition is challenging but achievable.  
- Reliable drug information retrieval requires robust backend architecture.  

## What's Next for PillPal

- Integrating voice assistance for hands-free interaction.
